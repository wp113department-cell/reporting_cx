📧 How to Create Gmail App Password ▼
ℹ️ Gmail App Password is a 16-character password that lets this app send emails on your behalf. You need 2-Step Verification enabled first.
  1. Go to myaccount.google.com and sign in with the Gmail you want to use.
  2. Click Security in the left menu.
  3. Under "How you sign in to Google", click 2-Step Verification. Enable it if not already on.
  4. Go back to Security → scroll down and find App passwords (it appears only after 2-Step Verification is on).
  5. Click App passwords. You may be asked to re-enter your password.
  6. In the "App name" field type: Daily Update App and click Create.
  7. Google shows a 16-character password (e.g. abcd efgh ijkl mnop). Copy it.
  8. Paste it into the Gmail App Password field above (without spaces).
  9. Click 🧪 Test Gmail Connection to verify it works.
⚠️ If you don't see "App passwords", make sure 2-Step Verification is fully enabled and you're not using a managed/workspace account with restrictions.
🔧 Fix Google 403 "Access Blocked" Error ▼
⚠️ You get this error when your Google email is not added as a test user in the Google Cloud Console project that owns the credentials.json.
  1. Open console.cloud.google.com and sign in with the Google account that owns the app (the account that created the project dailyupdates-500612).
  2. In the top bar, make sure the project dailyupdates-500612 is selected.
  3. In the left menu go to APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen.
  4. Scroll down to the Test users section.
  5. Click + Add users.
  6. Enter the work email you want to connect (e.g. wp113.department@gmail.com). Add all emails that will use this app.
  7. Click Save.
  8. Come back here and click Connect Google Drive again.
ℹ️ Only users added here can connect during testing. You can add up to 100 test users. If you need more, you can publish the app (requires Google verification).
☁️ How to Connect Google Drive ▼
ℹ️ This only needs to be done once. After connecting, the app saves your daily .txt and .docx files automatically to your work Google Drive.
  1. Make sure your email is added as a test user (see guide above if you got a 403 error).
  2. Make sure http://localhost:5000/oauth2callback is listed as an Authorized Redirect URI in your OAuth 2.0 Client:
    • Google Cloud Console → APIs & Services → Credentials
    • Click your OAuth 2.0 Client ID
    • Under "Authorized redirect URIs" → click + Add URI → type http://localhost:5000/oauth2callback
    • Click Save
  3. Click Connect Google Drive above.
  4. A Google login page opens. Select your work Google account.
  5. If you see a warning "Google hasn't verified this app" — click Advanced → Go to dailyupdates (unsafe). This is safe for your own private app.
  6. Click Allow to grant Drive access.
  7. You'll be redirected back here with a "✅ Connected" message.
